A small business minded multi disciplined team doing it all from intial Research & Design to Test-Driven Development & Support

Design First

Making Data Driven Decisions

When we start a new project at the Innovation Studio the effort is kicked off by initial user research and is followed by our complete User-Centered UX process. This initial research shapes our product direction so that our offering will enable users to effectively and intuitively accomplish what they need. This iterative process involves not only potential users, but also all the stakeholders including business partners, the technology team and leadership.

Agile

A Start-up Rich Mentality

50% of the Innovation Studio team comes from a Start-up background, a mentality that has proven to be contagious throughout the team. As a result, we approach each opportunity we have to improve through well-built software as a new business. This drives us to work closely with stakeholders, users and leadership throughout the entire process.

Experimentation

Not Afraid to Fail

The Innovation Studio embraces what has been traditionally seen as failure in IT. Our process encourages all members of the team to be constantly experimenting, testing and evaluating with our products, methodology and more. A fundamental principle of this team is that the process of creating the best possible product will and should include failures along the way.

Testing

Bet Your House On It

As our famous QA lead says “its not done until you can bet your house on it working”. Testing, in all its forms, Unit, Functional, Design Assessments, etc, has become expected behavior for everyone apart of the Innovation Studio. As a result we can proudly state that our phones never ring because our code has broken down.

Multi Disciplined

A Swiss Army Knife

Or to be more accurate a Swiss Army Knife full of Swiss Army Knives. From initial user research to QA testing and everything in-between the Innovation Studio has the skill set in house. This creates a dynamic, which is unique compared to traditional corporate IT teams. This allows constant collaboration between all the different concentrations on the team.
